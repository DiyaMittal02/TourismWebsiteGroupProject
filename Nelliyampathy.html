About Nelliyampathy

Nelliyampathy, ensconced in the Western Ghats of Kerala, India, is a hidden gem of natural splendor. This picturesque hill station is renowned for its lush tea and coffee plantations, mist-clad hills, and cascading waterfalls. Perched at an altitude of around 1,500 meters, Nelliyampathy offers a refreshing escape from the hustle and bustle of urban life.

The journey to Nelliyampathy is an adventure in itself, involving a scenic drive along hairpin bends and winding roads that provide breathtaking views of the Palakkad Gap. The region is a haven for nature enthusiasts, with opportunities for trekking amidst dense forests, exploring orange orchards, and relishing panoramic vistas from Seetharkundu Viewpoint.

Moreover, Nelliyampathy houses the Pothundi Reservoir, an ideal spot for boating and picnics. The serene Padagiri Peak beckons trekkers for a challenging yet rewarding climb. With its cool climate, misty landscapes, and verdant valleys, Nelliyampathy stands as an idyllic destination for those seeking tranquility amidst nature's bounties.

Things To Do

Nelliyampathy, nestled in the Western Ghats of Kerala, India, offers a tranquil escape into nature's embrace. Here are some things to do in Nelliyampathy:

1. Seetharkundu Viewpoint:

Enjoy panoramic views of the valley and forests from this viewpoint. It's an ideal spot for photography and to soak in the natural beauty.

2. Trekking:

Explore the lush greenery and forests through various trekking trails. The Padagiri and Kesavanpara treks are popular options.

3. Visit Tea Gardens:

Take a leisurely stroll through the sprawling tea estates, breathe in the fresh mountain air, and learn about the tea-making process.

4. Orange Orchards:

Visit the orange orchards and relish the juicy, freshly plucked fruits. This is a unique experience in Nelliyampathy.

5. Pothundi Reservoir:

Enjoy a boat ride on the serene waters of Pothundi Reservoir. It's a great place for a leisurely day out with family or friends.

6. Bike Riding:

Rent a bike and explore the scenic beauty of Nelliyampathy at your own pace. The winding roads and lush landscapes make for a memorable ride.

7. Waterfall Visit:

Visit the Nelliyampathy Waterfalls and take a refreshing dip in the natural pool. It's a great way to cool off and enjoy the natural surroundings.

8. Picnic at Manpara:

Manpara is a grassy meadow surrounded by forests, perfect for a picnic. Enjoy the serene ambiance and relax amidst nature.

9. Bird Watching:

The region is rich in birdlife. Bring binoculars and enjoy birdwatching in the lush forests of Nelliyampathy.

10. Photography:

Capture the breathtaking landscapes, tea gardens, waterfalls, and the overall natural beauty. Nelliyampathy provides ample opportunities for stunning photographs.

Remember to respect the natural surroundings and follow any local guidelines or regulations. Enjoy your visit to Nelliyampathy!

How To Reach

Nelliyampathy, located in the state of Kerala, India, is a serene hill station in the Western Ghats. To reach Nelliyampathy, you can consider the following options:

1. By Air:

- The nearest airport to Nelliyampathy is Coimbatore International Airport (IATA: CJB) in Tamil Nadu, approximately 120 kilometers away. From the airport, you can hire a taxi or take a bus to reach Nelliyampathy.

2. By Train:

- The nearest railway station is Palakkad Junction (station code: PUK), which is about 52 kilometers away. From the railway station, you can hire a taxi or take a local bus to reach Nelliyampathy.

3. By Road:

- Nelliyampathy is well-connected by road to major cities and towns in Kerala and neighboring states. You can either drive your own vehicle or hire a taxi to reach Nelliyampathy. The road leading to Nelliyampathy is scenic but can be winding, so take caution while driving.

4. By Bus:

- There are regular bus services from nearby towns and cities to Nelliyampathy. You can take a state-run or private bus to reach Nelliyampathy.

5. Local Transportation:

- Once you reach Nelliyampathy town, you can use local taxis or auto-rickshaws to get around and explore the nearby attractions.

6. Trekking from Palakkad:

- Another adventurous option is to trek to Nelliyampathy from Palakkad. This trek takes you through scenic trails and offers a unique perspective of the Western Ghats.

Remember to check for any updates or changes in transportation options, especially if you're traveling during holidays or special events. It's always a good idea to confirm schedules and availability before your journey. Enjoy your visit to Nelliyampathy!
