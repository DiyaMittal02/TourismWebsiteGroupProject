About Arvalem Caves

Arvalem Caves, also known as the Pandava Caves, are a fascinating historical and archaeological site nestled in the verdant landscapes of Goa, India. These rock-cut caves, believed to date back to the 6th or 7th century, provide a window into the region's rich past.

The Arvalem Caves are characterized by their simple yet striking architecture. The cave complex consists of five compartments, each adorned with intricate carvings and inscriptions that showcase a blend of Hindu and Buddhist influences. These cave chambers are believed to have been used for meditation and religious rituals.

In addition to the historical significance, the surrounding natural beauty adds to the charm of the site. Visitors can explore the caves, appreciating the stone carvings and the serene ambiance. The nearby Arvalem Waterfall, often referred to as the Harvalem Waterfall, further enhances the site's appeal, making it a must-visit destination for those seeking a mix of history, culture, and natural beauty in the heart of Goa.
